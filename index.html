The Glossary

Qubit

The basic unit of quantum information. Unlike a binary bit (0 or 1), a qubit can exist in multiple states at once thanks to superposition.

Superposition

A fundamental principle where a quantum system can be in multiple states simultaneously until it is observed or measured.

Entanglement

Einstein called this "spooky action at a distance." Two particles become linked so that measuring one instantly tells you the state of the other, no matter the distance.

Decoherence

The "enemy" of quantum computers. It happens when outside heat or vibration causes the qubit to lose its quantum state and turn back into a normal bit.
